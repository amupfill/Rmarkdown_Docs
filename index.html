Pakistan Poliovirus Population Immunity

This page is a example of an R Markdown page, using Pakistan Poliovirus Population Immunity as an example.

Immunity trends since 2001

We estimate immunity (probability of protection against paralysis) for each non-polio AFP case between age 6 to 59 months using reported OPV dose history and the history of polio vaccination campaigns in each cases district of residence. Below, the avegage immunity is plotted over time for the whole country.

temp = aggregate(n[101], n[109], mean, na.rm=T)
ggplot(temp, aes(x=year.6mo, y=type1_immunity)) + geom_line() + geom_point() + 
  labs(y="Mean Type 1 Immunity",x="Six-Month Interval") + 
  scale_y_continuous(limit=c(0,1)) + theme_prefered()

District immunity estimates

In order to estimate population immunity at the district-level, we smoothed estimates using a spatiotemporal model to reduce noise arising from small samples sizes (each district often has less than 10 NP-AFP cases in a six-month interval). Below, a histogram of smoothed district immunity estimates from 2014.

ggplot(preds[preds$year==2014,], aes(x=type1_immunity)) + geom_histogram(binwidth=.05) +
  labs(x="Estimated Type 1 Immunity", y="District Count") + theme_prefered()
